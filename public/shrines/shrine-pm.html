i've recently started collecting physical copies of all the music & movies i like. being able to access everything online is great but i also like holding Things in my Hands. still a wip

ABBA Gold: Greatest Hits (1992) — ABBA

a classic. one of my core memories is associated with this album. my wonderfully kind and eccentric fifth grade english teacher would have us take breaks when she felt the class getting too bored with the lesson and would play Dancing Queen and teach us a dance to it that she'd make us all perform at random points throughout the year.

i bought this from goodwill! i love browsing through their music selection. they mostly have christmas music or like... vague coffee shop music but if i'm lucky i find something good. the following scans are from the booklet. a little bit of abba history and some cool photo collages!

Welcome To The Neighborhood (1995) — Meat Loaf

found this gem at goodwill the week i got back home from my trip to the summer, so i saw this as a Good Omen for sure. i haven't listened to much meat loaf but from what i've listened to so far, i really love. dad rock hits like nothing else, truly.

favorite tracks: If This Is The Last Kiss & Original Sin

i was so charmed by the booklet that came with this CD. each track and lyrics page has their own book cover design illustration that i'm just so obsessed with.

it's hard for me to explain it, but meat loaf's songs have such strong narratives, i just get so immersed in the stories each song has to offer, which is something i really love. i group this album with glass animals' how to be a human being album in the songs i can visually See category. and these book cover illustrations that are included in the booklet totally play into that! i just love this CD so much... excellent packaging. such a fun concept. a work of Art...

On the Radio: Greatest Hits Volumes I & II (1979) — Donna Summer

a queen! got this from a record store near where i live, and i'm already looking forward to going back. i used to listen to a lot of Donna Summer in high school, so i love being able to have this collection in my hands. can't go wrong with the Greatest Hits. i'm listening to it while i type this now...

favorite tracks: Last Dance & Bad Girls

Oops! I Did It Again (2000) — Britney Spears

yeah, sure, maybe a big reason for this new physical media collecting hobby of mine is nostalgia. sue me! this britney CD brings me back... i loved her as a kid, and i still love her now! i still have yet to read her book... i've been meaning to for a while. the booklet for this CD had a bunch of really adorable photos, i scanned a couple of my favorites.

favorite tracks: What U See (Is What U Get) & Don't Go Knockin' on My Door

Never Mind the Bollocks, Here's the Sex Pistols (1977) — Sex Pistols

this is my first sex pistols album, actually! i've heard about them talked about a lot, especially when i was balls deep into my Nana phase, but i've never actually given them a listen.

Not Friends Special Edition (2021) — LOONA

my very first LOONA album... from some random person selling k-pop merch on instagram, i think. LOONA was my introduction to k-pop and my favorite color and i really like the color red, so did i really have any other choice? loved this photobook, i loved the concept for this single. i just loved seeing trigger happy Heejin run around shoot up the place to save her girlfriends.

favorite track: (there's literally just one song... in different versions. call me basic but the original Not Friends is my favorite)

Formula of Love: O+T=＜3 (Study About Love Ver.) (2021) — TWICE

i consider myself a casual TWICE enjoyer but it's funny because every time they release something new i'm always like huh i actually love this maybe i'll get the album CD to listen to at home... and i end up buying the album. i don't know what to do with all the photocards i have of these girls because i don't know any of their names... i just really like their tunes... lol.

favorite tracks: MOONLIGHT & F.I.L.A. (Fall In Love Again)

so groovy...

HULA HOOP / StarSeed ～カクセイ (2021) — LOONA

this was the album that made me realize how much more expensive japanese releases are in k-pop. crazy. i did appreciate that this also came with a DVD! which i actually didn't watch until literally today (and i've had this for a couple years... i just didn't have a DVD player until recently) which is funny. the DVD was like an episode of one of those videos where they have the girlies do sports and activities like tug-a-war and stuff at some gym. just gals havin' fun. except this had no subtitles so i didn't understand a word they were saying. hula hoops were thrown! and hooped!

favorite tracks: HULA HOOP (duh) & StarSeed (also duh)

FEARLESS [Japanese Version] (2022) — Le Sserafim

i'm going to be honest i don't even know why i bought the japanese version of this single album... i don't even like the japanese version that much more than the original korean, and i'm pretty sure i impulse bought this without reading it close enough. i should probably sell or gift this honestly...

favorite track: FEARLESS (Japanese ver.)

Ready to Be (Ready Ver.) (2023) — TWICE

gorgeous mini album from the twice girls.... got this one from target. not much to say really i just listened to the album on streaming and liked it so much i had to get the real thing in my hands. i play this a lot... good background music for when i just wanna hang out in my room...

favorite tracks: MOONLIGHT SUNRISE & WALLFLOWER

Version Up (JINSOUL Ver.) (2023) — Odd Eye Circle

after all the LOONA troubles, i was so excited to see Odd Eye Circle come back with a new mini album under their new company. i was so glad to see this new more mature concept from them. they all looked so cool in their dark suits and leather and the eye patches!!! i figure it's a callback to their loona lore i love that they're still keeping it going... such a fun album too. air force one was stuck in my head for weeks back in summer '23.

favorite tracks: Air Force One & Je Ne Sais Quoi

The World EP.2: Outlaw (2023) — ATEEZ

'slow it downnn make it bouncy' changed lives. my favorite release from them so far, honestly. i think it's the only full album in all of k-pop where i don't skip a single song... which is very rare. can't think of another one off the top of my head right now so. good job ateez.

favorite tracks: DUNE & DJANGO

i apologize for the lack of photobook scans for this one because i really didn't want to bend the book too much. wanted to keep it nice and in one piece for at least a while longer... but the comic book concept in the beginning pages was so fun and creative!

FL!P THAT (A ver.) (2022) — LOONA

loona's last album release before they disbanded (thank god... at least they're finally free from that wretched company). such a cute fun summertime album i really loved this album a lot. and it feels like a good way to end this Chapter of their Lives... as dramatic as that may sound. gettin' on that magic train to freedom! lmao.

favorite tracks: Flip That & Pale Blue Dot

a tragic event happen while i was flipping through flip that... the pages flipped out and tore from the binding! (i tried a little alliteration...) which is actually pretty disappointing given i rarely ever open these photo books and i have never bent or flattened this one in particular, so i was really surprised (in a not so good way) that the pages started falling out. but hey, at least these scans look great for the most part! loona's last group album deteriorating as the group fell apart is poetic, in a sense...

LOVE DIVE (Ver. 2) (2022) — LOONA

i totally forgot i had this still until today. honestly i love this EP. both songs feel like they should be in a movie soundtrack. if the main character was a superstar. you know? anyways, i think the main reason i bought this was one was the nice poster i have up on my wall.

favorite tracks: (literally all 2 of them) ROYAL & LOVE DIVE

r.i.p. to the spine of this photobook. it's fine, but it hurt me physically and emotionally to flatten it to scan.

CLANNAD ORIGINAL SOUNDTRACK (2004) — published by VISUAL ARTS Co.

as if the series didn't emotionally devastate me enough (i mean that in a positive sense) i now have the full original soundtrack to CLANNAD! this is such a lovely package, and was so happy when it finally arrived. three CDs, filled with beautiful soft tunes. i do still have to pause myself and Take a Seat when i hear a version of the dango song... it still makes me want to cry every time i hear it.

favorite tracks: A Couple of Idiots & Nagisa

i ended up finding this cool site with full scans of what's inside the booklet and all the tracks if you'd like to listen to the full soundtrack yourself! they also include full details on who composed each song and all that good stuff. worth checking out! they have a ton of other video game soundtracks too (it's where i downloaded all the silent hill music from so i can listen on my phone heheh)
